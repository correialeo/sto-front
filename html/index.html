OCEANS 20

Know more

The O20, which had its embryo in the last two G20 cycles, in Indonesia and India, will have an important role in giving a voice to civil society, considering non-governmental organizations, private initiatives, indigenous peoples, traditional communities and scientists. The group strengthens and consolidates the presence of the ocean in the G20, by integrating the countries that preceded and will be Brazil in its presidency, and the Sherpa Trails and Finance Working Groups.


The creation of Oceans20 by the Brazilian Presidency at the G20 is a historic milestone in recognizing the central role of the ocean in the global climate, energy and environment agendas and that, despite being a global and interconnected ocean, it is plural in its characteristics.


The ocean agenda is one of the four pillars of the Working Group on Climate and Environmental Sustainability, coordinated by the Ministry of the Environment, and permeates other working groups, given the transversal nature of the topic. Oceans 20 is coordinated by the UNESCO Chair for Ocean Sustainability at the University of São Paulo, in collaboration with the World Economic Forum, the UN Global Compact – Rede Brasil and Ocean Stewardship Coalition -, the Brazilian Biodiversity Fund (FUNBIO) and the National Institute for Ocean Research (INPO).

LIMPEZA DOS MARES


The Limpeza dos Mares Project is an initiative that aims to raise awareness about the importance of preserving the oceans and marine life. Since 2014, in 10 seasons and 40 stages, the project has removed more than 165.3 tons of waste from the seabed, beaches and shores with the support of dolphin, turtle and starfish volunteers (categories of volunteers).
